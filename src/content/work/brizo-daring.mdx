{/* STANDFIRST */}

Editorial production for Brizo, published in Sixtysix Magazine.

{/* BRIEF */}

Brizo set out to launch Daring as a kitchen and bath collection that would pull the category out of utility and into the language that interior designers and architects already speak. The brief was less about introducing fixtures than about positioning them, on the terms of a design audience that has long treated plumbing as the last bastion of the merely functional. Brizo wanted Daring read as objects, not specifications.

{/* APPROACH */}

We built the feature around a single residential setting that gave each fixture room to be looked at the way a piece of furniture is looked at. Photography by [Photographer] worked tight and tactile, treating each fixture as material first, function second. Set design by [Stylist] placed the pieces in dialogue with period furniture and contemporary ceramics, deliberately avoiding the showroom staging that defines most of the category. Words by [Writer] framed the line through the design lead's intent and the longer arc of bath fixtures as objects of taste, in a register closer to a design publication than a brand book.

{/* OUTPUT */}

The feature ran in Sixtysix Magazine across [page count] pages and was extended into Brizo's KBIS launch assets for the season.
